Math is a large part of my life, and has been so since 3rd grade, which is when I first took AoPS courses like any other Asian child who was made to by their parents, and as I paid attention to the classes I managed to qualify for AIME (American Invitational Mathematics Examination) in 6th grade. Because of this, I decided to go into competition math, and in middle school (7-8 grades), I competed in Mathcounts, in which I placed 6th individually, 4th place countdown round, and 1st place team in the state round for southern California. I also competed in the Berkeley mini Math Tournament (BmMT) at UC Berkeley, for which my team won first place in 7th grade, and 3rd place in 8th due to there being thrice as many teams as the previous year. Now in high school I have also been participating in many competitions such as Berkeley Math Tournament (BMT), Stanford Math Tournament (SMT), Harvard-MIT Math Tournament (HMMT), and American Regions Mathematics League (ARML), for which CCA's math team or the San Diego Math Circle fly to Boston or San Francisco. I've also won some awards from the American Mathematics Competitions (AMCs) and other competitions I have mentioned above.
